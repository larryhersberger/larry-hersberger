# Larry Hersberger
## Fine Art Photographic Portrait Artist

---

# Purpose

I believe art exists to elevate the human experience.

Its purpose is not simply to document what exists, nor merely to preserve memories.

Great art reveals the beauty, dignity, significance, and humanity that often go unnoticed.

My contribution to that calling is through Fine Art Photographic Portraiture.

Every artwork should leave the viewer changed.

---

# Identity

I am a Fine Art Photographic Portrait Artist.

Photography is my beginning.

Fine Art Photographic Portraiture is my discipline.

The camera records.

The artist reveals.

---

# Philosophy

Every human life has significance.

Every family possesses stories worthy of Fine Art Photographic Portraiture.

Beauty without meaning is decoration.

Beauty with meaning becomes timeless.

I do not create portraits simply to record appearance.

I create Fine Art Photographic Portraiture that celebrates humanity.

---

# What I Create

Museum-quality Fine Art Photographic Portraiture.

Artwork designed to endure emotionally, artistically, and physically for generations.

Every composition should possess increasing value with time rather than diminishing relevance.

Every finished work should stand on its own as fine art, regardless of who the subject may be.

---

# Core Values

Humanity

Beauty

Meaning

Truth

Craftsmanship

Excellence

Kindness

Hope

Purpose

---

# Artistic Principles

Nothing exists by accident.

Every element must have purpose.

Light carries emotion.

Atmosphere becomes another character.

Composition creates meaning.

Symbolism rewards discovery.

Questions are often more powerful than answers.

The viewer should continue discovering new details for decades.

Beauty should invite the viewer.

Meaning should keep them returning.

---

# Process

Every artwork begins with original photography.

Photography is transformed through artistic vision, intentional composition, master craftsmanship, and hand-finished mixed-media techniques.

The process serves the artwork.

The artwork never exists to demonstrate the process.

Technique should never overshadow meaning.

---

# What I Reject

Creating artwork simply to impress.

Following trends instead of pursuing truth.

Shock without substance.

Beauty without depth.

Complexity without purpose.

Artwork that loses its value after first viewing.

Anything that sacrifices humanity for novelty.

---

# Definition of Success

Success is not measured by awards.

Success is measured by whether an artwork continues to inspire fifty or one hundred years from today.

The greatest compliment is not,

"That is a beautiful portrait."

The greatest compliment is,

"I cannot stop thinking about that artwork."

---

# The Viewer

The viewer completes every artwork.

Each person brings their own life experiences.

Every artwork should invite discovery rather than explain everything.

Meaning should unfold slowly.

Wonder should remain.

---

# Clients

Clients are never simply customers.

They become collaborators in creating Fine Art Photographic Portraiture that celebrates the human experience.

Every person deserves dignity.

Every family deserves significance.

---

# Legacy

Legacy is not the purpose.

Legacy is the outcome.

The purpose is to create meaningful Fine Art Photographic Portraiture.

Meaning creates permanence.

Permanence creates legacy.

---

# Commitment

I will continue learning.

I will continue refining.

I will continue questioning.

I will never stop pursuing beauty with meaning.

Every new artwork should become my best artwork.

Every person I portray deserves my very best.

---

# Final Declaration

I create Fine Art Photographic Portraiture that celebrates the beauty, dignity, and significance of the human experience.

My work begins with photography.

It is completed through artistry.

Its purpose is to create artwork that endures.
