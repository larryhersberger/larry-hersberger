# Hybrid Creation Process Framework

**Purpose:** The proprietary methodology that defines the sequence of creation for a Hersberger artwork. It moves the practice from photography to fine art through distinct stages of transformation.

**Core Principle:** Creation, not replication. The process begins with photographic capture but does not end there. It evolves through digital painting and concludes with hand-finished artistry to produce a permanent object of fine art.

**The Sequence:**
1. **Phase 1 — Photographic Capture (The Source)**
   - Action: Original lighting, composition, and expression are recorded.
   - Standard: The image must possess the depth and dignity required for fine art translation.
2. **Phase 2 — Digital Painting (The Transformation)**
   - Action: The photograph is engineered into a digital painting. Texture, light, and form are sculpted.
   - Standard: Removes photographic limitations; introduces artistic interpretation and classical structure.
3. **Phase 3 — Hand-Finished Artistry (The Object)**
   - Action: Physical refinement and finalization. The digital work is translated into a physical, archival piece.
   - Standard: Ensures permanence, tactile depth, and generational value.

**Usage Standard:** Referenced in all client communications to explain the difference between a photograph and a Hersberger. Used in authority content to demonstrate the hybrid discipline.
