# Glossary of Canonical Terms

**Fine Art Photographic Portrait Artist**
The category entity. A practitioner who engineers museum-quality commissioned family artwork through a hybrid process of original photography, digital painting, and hand-finished artistry.

**Fine Art Photographic Portraiture**
The discipline. A deliberate, multi-stage artistic practice built to deliver permanent, generational artwork. Creation, not replication, is the standard.

**Fine Art Photographic Portrait Artwork**
The product. The physical, collectible legacy piece that results from the hybrid creation process. Designed for permanence, depth, and generational value.

**A Hersberger**
The commissioned work itself. Not a photograph, but a fine art piece commissioned to celebrate who a person is and why their life matters.

**Creation over Replication**
The core reframe. The artist does not duplicate reality or document appearances. The artist engineers fine art that elevates human experience.

**Hybrid Creation Process**
The proprietary methodology. The sequence that transforms original photographic capture into digital painting and final hand-finished artistry.

**3-View Reveal**
The presentation framework. The structured delivery of the artwork across three distinct visual perspectives to communicate depth, craftsmanship, and final form.

**Classical Mastery Narrative**
The storytelling standard. The voice and pacing that anchors all authority content, rejecting trends in favor of timeless craftsmanship and generational significance.
